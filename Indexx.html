The Significance of Guru Purnima

Guru Purnima is a sacred festival celebrated to honor spiritual and academic teachers. It is celebrated on the full moon day in the Hindu month of Ashadha, as it is known in the Hindu calendar.

Swami Vivekananda - A guiding light for millions

The festival is common to many spiritual traditions in Hinduism, where it is dedicated to the expression of gratitude towards the teacher by their disciple. The festival is celebrated by Buddhists in honor of the Buddha who gave his first sermon on this day at Sarnath, Uttar Pradesh, India.

"The guru is the bright mask which God wears in order to come to us. As we look steadily on, gradually the mask falls off and God is revealed."
- Swami Vivekananda

This day marks the first peak of the lunar cycle after the peak of the solar cycle. The spiritual significance of the day is that when the disciple is ready, the master appears. The day is observed by offering respect to one's gurus, teachers and mentors.
